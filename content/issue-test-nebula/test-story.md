Elara pressed her palm against the moss-covered wall. The stone pulsed with warmth, responding to her touch. She had discovered this secret passage three days ago, and each night since, she'd returned to explore deeper.

The corridor opened into a vast chamber. Crystals jutted from every surface, refracting moonlight into a thousand dancing colors. In the center stood a pedestal, and upon it—

***

—a single golden key.

She reached for it, fingers trembling. The moment she touched the metal, the chamber transformed. The crystals blazed with inner light, and voices echoed from everywhere and nowhere.

"Finally," they whispered. "The labyrinth keeper has returned."

***

Elara stumbled backward. The key burned in her hand, not with heat, but with purpose. Through the walls, she could see them now—countless paths branching through impossible space.

The labyrinth had been waiting for her all along.
