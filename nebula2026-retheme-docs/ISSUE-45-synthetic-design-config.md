# Issue 45 — Synthetic Design Configuration

> Generated by Claude Opus 4.6 during the nebula2026 retheme project, April 2026.
> These colour schemes and roundel assignments were derived from visual analysis of each story's artwork and thematic reading of the editorial descriptions.

---

## Cover Art — Monkey King

The cover is a traditional East Asian illustration of the Monkey King in flowing, swirling clouds. The palette is warm throughout: ochres, terracotta, sage green, and cream, with the Monkey King's form picked out in cinnabar orange-red.

Three schemes were proposed:

| Scheme | Primary | Secondary | Accent | Feel |
|--------|---------|-----------|--------|------|
| **A. Warm Ochre** | `#8b6914` | `#c4883a` | `#3d2b08` | Gold-forward, emphasises the swirling clouds |
| **B. Sage & Terra** | `#5c6b4a` | `#b8622e` | `#2a3020` | Green-earth tone with terracotta warmth |
| **C. Cinnabar** | `#a04828` | `#c89a5c` | `#4a1a0a` | Led by the orange-red of the Monkey King's form |

**Chosen: C (Cinnabar), darkened** to `#6e2e18` / `#9a6a3a` / `#2e0e04` — the editor preferred the cinnabar direction but wanted it richer and more grounded, less bright. The darkened version sits better against the cream tones of the artwork without competing with it.

## Per-Story Colour Schemes

Each story page can override the issue-level colour scheme via `colorScheme` frontmatter. The three tokens are:

- **primary** — headings, borders, nav strip, author photo border, Basalte Fond base layer
- **secondary** — Basalte Volume overlay layer, secondary UI elements
- **accent** — text-shadow on chromatic titles, feather gradients

### The Agentic Necklace
**Image:** Scorched red earth, terracotta bricks, sand, a small blade half-buried in dust.
```yaml
colorScheme:
  primary: "#8b3422"    # terracotta red — dominant colour of the baked earth
  secondary: "#c47a3a"  # sandy ochre — the lighter dust and sand tones
  accent: "#3e1208"     # dark burnt umber — the deep shadows between the rocks
```
The image is almost monochromatic in its red-earth palette. The scheme leans into that, pulling the terracotta for primary and letting the sandy highlights provide contrast through the secondary.

### The Negotiation
**Image:** Deep green palm forest with long shadows, a woman in a cobalt blue dress walking among the trees, a blue-spotted butterfly on a pale trunk.
```yaml
colorScheme:
  primary: "#1a4d2e"    # forest green — the overwhelming colour of the canopy and grass
  secondary: "#2444a0"  # cobalt blue — the woman's dress and the butterfly's wings
  accent: "#0a2414"     # dark woodland — the deep shadow between the palm trunks
```
Two strong colours dominate this image: green and blue. Rather than blending them, the scheme keeps them distinct — green for structure (primary), blue for the striking visual accents (secondary). The story is horror set in a seemingly beautiful place; the dark accent reinforces the shadows lurking beneath.

### On Miracles
**Image:** Dreamy, ethereal composition. A woman lying on white sheets, soft mint-green and cream tones, overexposed and intimate.
```yaml
colorScheme:
  primary: "#5c8a82"    # muted sage-teal — the subtle green wash across the image
  secondary: "#9aaca6"  # pale green-grey — the lightest tones, almost silver
  accent: "#2a4a44"     # deep teal — grounding shadow beneath the softness
```
This is the quietest image in the issue. The scheme respects that — all three values are muted and close in tone, creating a gentle, contemplative atmosphere. The story is described as having "the faintest of magic realist touches," and the palette mirrors that subtlety.

### The Perfect Mother
**Image:** Pop art close-up — hot pink hair, rainbow headband, white oversized sunglasses, multicoloured nails, red drips from the lens. Bold, loud, unsettling.
```yaml
colorScheme:
  primary: "#b82858"    # hot pink — the dominant hair colour and overall vibe
  secondary: "#d46a4a"  # coral-red — the warm skin tones and red drip accents
  accent: "#5a0a24"     # dark magenta — deep shadow behind the spectacle
```
The most vibrant image in the issue demands the most saturated scheme. The hot pink primary is a deliberate departure from the muted tones elsewhere — this story is horror dressed up in candy colours, and the scheme should feel slightly wrong, slightly too bright. The dark magenta accent anchors it.

### Pultu's Lesson
**Image:** Painterly forest scene — a massive wild boar's face fills the frame, a small golden bird perched on its head, rich greens and yellows, dappled light.
```yaml
colorScheme:
  primary: "#4a6a1e"    # woodland green — the forest canopy and undergrowth
  secondary: "#b89a2a"  # golden yellow — the bird, the dappled sunlight, the boar's bristles
  accent: "#1e2e0a"     # deep forest — the darkest shadows in the trees
```
The image has a fairy-tale quality — the bird-on-boar composition feels like an illustration from a fable, which matches the story's "lesson" framing. Green and gold are the natural pair here, with the gold secondary adding warmth that the Negotiation's forest scheme deliberately lacks.

### Search History
**Image:** Dark, digitally distorted texture — blue and amber waves ripple across a black background, white text fragments of search queries visible through the distortion.
```yaml
colorScheme:
  primary: "#1a4a6a"    # deep digital blue — the dominant cool tone
  secondary: "#3a7a9a"  # electric teal-blue — the brighter wave crests
  accent: "#0a1e2e"     # near-black blue — the void behind the distortion
```
The darkest scheme in the issue, matching the darkest image. The story is told entirely through search queries — no prose, no narrator — and the scheme reflects that stark, clinical quality. All three values are cool blues with no warmth, mirroring the inhuman digital medium.

### The Spear Cuts Through Water (review)
**Image:** Split composition — author photo (grey tones) on the left, book cover on the right showing luminous aqua-green light through dark blue-teal foliage, silhouetted figures.
```yaml
colorScheme:
  primary: "#0e5e5a"    # deep teal — the dark foliage framing the cover art
  secondary: "#44b896"  # bright aqua-green — the luminous central glow
  accent: "#042e2c"     # dark sea — the deepest shadows
```
The book cover's striking aqua glow against dark teal is the clear palette source. The high contrast between primary and secondary gives this scheme more energy than the other cool-toned stories — appropriate for what the reviewer calls "the performance of a lifetime."

### Short Reviews
**Image:** A vast impossible library — towering shelves forming a canyon, warm amber light flooding from the far end, a lone silhouette, paper pages flying through the air.
```yaml
colorScheme:
  primary: "#5a3e1e"    # warm umber — the wooden shelves and deep shadows
  secondary: "#a87a3e"  # amber gold — the dramatic backlighting
  accent: "#2a1a0a"     # deep shadow brown — the darkness of the stacks
```
The warmest scheme after the cover, befitting an image that's essentially a love letter to books. The amber secondary captures the cinematic shaft of light that draws the eye (and the silhouette) into the depths. Warm browns ground the fantasy in something that feels like old wood and leather.

## Roundel Assignments

The nebula2026 theme has 10 hand-designed SVG roundels. Each story can specify a `chapterMarker` in frontmatter to use as its chapter-break divider. The roundels are abstract/symbolic, so the assignment is interpretive rather than literal.

Available roundels: MythaxisAbduction, MythaxisEye, MythaxisGalaxy, MythaxisGrey, MythaxisHand, MythaxisIcon (brand), MythaxisKnot, MythaxisMonster, MythaxisSwords, MythaxisTarget.

| Story | Roundel | Reasoning |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| **The Agentic Necklace** | **MythaxisKnot** | The story spans years, following the intertwined bond between a man and a sentient necklace. The Celtic knot — loops without beginning or end — mirrors both the physical form of a necklace and the entangled relationship at the story's heart. |
| **The Negotiation** | **MythaxisEye** | A horror story set in a beautiful forest, where something is watching. The eye roundel captures the unsettling sense of observation that pervades the piece — the feeling of being seen by something you can't quite identify. |
| **On Miracles** | **MythaxisGalaxy** | Described as having "the faintest of magic realist touches," this literary story deals in quiet wonder. The spiral galaxy suggests something vast and mysterious operating just beyond the edges of the everyday — miracles glimpsed, not grasped. |
| **The Perfect Mother** | **MythaxisHand** | A horror story about family and motherhood. The reaching hand is ambiguous — nurturing or grasping, protective or possessive — which is exactly the tension the story explores. The editor notes it as a "family-life bookend" to an earlier story; hands connect across that gap. |
| **Pultu's Lesson** | **MythaxisMonster** | A fantasy fable centred on a wild boar — the beast in the forest, the creature that teaches the lesson. The monster roundel is the most direct match in the set: the story's heart is the encounter with something wild and powerful and other. |
| **Search History** | **MythaxisGrey** | Told entirely through search queries with no human narrator, this sci-fi story implies an inhuman intelligence sifting through digital remains. The alien grey — a face that's almost but not quite human — captures the uncanny valley of an AI or post-human entity piecing together a life from its search history. |
| **The Spear Cuts Through Water** | **MythaxisSwords** | A review of an epic fantasy novel about a quest involving combat, gods, and a broken world. The crossed swords are the most literal match: the spear of the title, the battles described, the genre's martial tradition. Sometimes the obvious choice is the right one. |
| **Short Reviews** | **MythaxisTarget** | A curated roundup — the reviewer sifting through months of short fiction to hit specific marks worth recommending. The target roundel suggests precision, selection, and aim. Each review is an arrow finding its book. |

## Issue Roundel

The `issueRoundel` is the issue-wide default roundel, distinct from per-story `chapterMarker` assignments. It appears in the story footer divider, the minimal sticky header, and the nav panel bottom. It represents the issue as a whole rather than any individual story.

> **Note:** Site-level pages (contents, about, submissions, catalogue listings, taxonomy pages) always use the brand roundel (`brandRoundel` from `config.yaml`, currently MythaxisIcon) in both divider positions — they are not tied to any specific issue.

**Chosen: MythaxisHand**

The cover art is the Monkey King — Sun Wukong — a mythic trickster from Chinese legend, depicted mid-flight through swirling clouds, reaching forward with dynamic energy. The reaching hand is the most fitting issue roundel for several reasons:

1. **The Monkey King's nature.** He is defined by reaching — reaching for immortality, reaching for heaven, reaching beyond his station. The grasping hand captures that restless ambition.
2. **Kinetic energy.** The cover art is all motion and flow. Of the ten roundels, the hand is the most dynamic — it implies action, not observation (Eye) or stillness (Target).
3. **Ambiguity.** The hand can read as grasping, offering, beckoning, or warning. That ambiguity suits an issue that spans sci-fi, horror, fantasy, and literary fiction — no single tone dominates.
4. **Not duplicated.** MythaxisHand is assigned to The Perfect Mother as a per-story `chapterMarker`, but the issue roundel and chapter markers serve different purposes in different contexts, so the overlap is acceptable. (The story's hand is maternal/possessive; the issue's hand is mythic/aspirational.)
5. **Editorial instinct confirmed.** Andrew had `MythaxisHand` pencilled in as a comment in the frontmatter before this analysis — independent convergence on the same choice suggests the match is genuine.

## Design Principles

A few notes on the approach taken:

1. **Image-led, not genre-led.** Colour schemes were derived from the dominant tones in each story's artwork, not from genre conventions (e.g. "horror = red"). This means the horror stories have very different palettes — forest green for The Negotiation, hot pink for The Perfect Mother — because their images are different.

2. **Contrast within the issue.** The schemes were designed to feel distinct when browsing the frontpage, where story cards alternate. Adjacent stories should not blur into each other. The warm terracotta of Agentic Necklace sits next to the cool green of Negotiation; the pastel quiet of On Miracles follows the loud pink of Perfect Mother.

3. **Accent as shadow.** In every scheme, the accent value is a much darker version of the primary — typically 60-70% darker. This is because the accent token drives the `text-shadow` on chromatic Basalte titles, and a too-light shadow looks muddy rather than dimensional.

4. **Roundels as metaphor.** The roundel assignments prioritise thematic resonance over literal illustration. MythaxisGalaxy doesn't mean the story is about space — it means the story deals in wonder. MythaxisGrey doesn't mean aliens appear — it means something inhuman is present.
